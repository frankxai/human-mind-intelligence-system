# Emotion — research grounding

> Non-clinical. This grounds the emotion construct model. It does not profile, score, or diagnose a person.

## The model

Emotion is the affective state that colors processing and pushes action. In the ontology it is a heavy modulator: it modulates attention, memory, motivation, and decision-making, and is itself modulated by social-cognition. It supplies no content of its own — it shifts the parameters of the constructs it touches.

## The prediction frame

The named anchor here is predicting emotional response to viewed content. Appraisal theory makes this computational: a situation is appraised against goals, and a predicted affect follows. The dimensional valence-arousal lens makes that prediction measurable along two axes. This grounds the model of how emotion is structured and elicited — it never reads a person. The affective-computing anchor models emotion *elicited by media*, cited per CANON.md's non-waivable boundary, not as a tool for profiling viewers.
