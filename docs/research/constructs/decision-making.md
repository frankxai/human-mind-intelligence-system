# Decision-making — research grounding

> Non-clinical. This grounds the decision-making construct model. It does not profile, score, or diagnose a person.

## The model

Decision-making is selecting among options under uncertainty. In the ontology it is a convergence node: emotion, motivation, and belief all feed or modulate it, and it feeds behavior. Metacognition reflects-on it. It is where the upstream constructs resolve into a committed choice.

## The prediction frame

Predictive processing frames choice as expected-free-energy minimization — picking the action whose predicted outcome best fits preferred states. Prospect theory and dual-process models, the schema's own lenses, sit inside that frame as descriptions of how real choice departs from the normative baseline. Because emotion, belief, motivation, and metacognition all feed this node, decision-making is where their parameters combine; the System predicts the likely choice with confidence and leaves the actual decision to the human.

## Anchors

- **An Introduction to Predictive Processing Models of Perception and Decision-Making** — Sprevak & Smith, Topics in Cognitive Science (2024). Accessible, current synthesis of predictive processing across perception and decision-making. [link](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/tops.12704)
- **Bayesian approaches to brain function (active inference, free energy)** — Friston et al., review (2010). Perception and action as Bayesian inference; action selected to minimize expected free energy. Grounds the motivation/behavior/belief edges. [link](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bayesian_approaches_to_brain_function)

## Lens alignment

Schema lenses: `expected-utility`, `prospect-theory`, `dual-process`, `ecological-rationality`, `implementation-intentions`. The predictive-processing anchor informs the prediction-first reading of `dual-process` and `ecological-rationality`; the Bayesian/free-energy anchor informs `expected-utility` (action over expected outcomes). `prospect-theory` and `implementation-intentions` come from the construct module.

## Boundary

This grounds a model of how choices are made. It does not license judging the wisdom, rationality, or risk of a person's actual decisions.
