# Identity — research grounding

> Non-clinical. This grounds the identity construct model. It does not profile, score, or diagnose a person.

## The model

Identity is the self-model — the stable account a person holds of who they are. In the ontology it is fed by memory and social-cognition, and it modulates behavior and motivation. It is a prior, not a content store: it shifts how action and effort are shaped without supplying their material.

## The prediction frame

The self-model can be read as a stable, narrative prior the system maintains and defends against revision. Work on LLM psychometrics probes whether models carry stable, trait-like profiles across prompts — a cautionary mirror for how we model identity, since the same methods carry serious validity caveats. We treat trait-stability as a modeling lens, not a verdict about any person's fixed character.

## Anchors

- **Large Language Model Psychometrics: A Systematic Review** — review, arXiv 2505.08245 (2025). How mental-construct measurement is being applied to models; relevant to identity/metacognition framing, with validity caveats. [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.08245)
- **Understanding World or Predicting Future? A Survey of World Models** — Survey, arXiv 2411.14499 (2024). Map of the world-model landscape; situates JEPA, Cosmos, and prediction-vs-generation debates. [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.14499)

## Lens alignment

Schema lenses: `erikson-stages`, `social-identity-theory`, `narrative-identity`, `self-discrepancy-theory`. The LLM-psychometrics review informs the trait-stability reading behind `self-discrepancy-theory` (measured self vs. ideal/ought) and `social-identity-theory`; the world-models survey informs the stable-prior reading behind `narrative-identity`. `erikson-stages` comes from the construct module.

## Boundary

This grounds a model of the self-concept. It does not license assigning a person a personality type, trait score, or fixed identity from observed behavior.
