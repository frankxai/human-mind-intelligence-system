# Social-cognition — research grounding

> Non-clinical. This grounds the social-cognition construct model. It does not profile, score, or diagnose a person.

## The model

Social-cognition is the modeling of other minds — inferring intentions, beliefs, and states behind others' behavior. In the ontology it feeds identity and modulates belief, behavior, and emotion. It is the construct that links a person's inner model to the social world around them.

## The prediction frame

Theory of mind — modeling other minds — is the construct frontier labs probe most directly in machines. Kosinski's false-belief battery measures that capacity in LLMs and reports GPT-4-class performance near older-child levels, treated as a measurement result with known training-contamination caveats, not proof of mind. LLM-psychometrics work situates these measurements and their validity limits. We borrow the framing of social inference as prediction over hidden states; we make no claim that models possess a theory of mind.

## Boundary

This grounds a model of how people infer other minds. It does not license assessing a person's social skill, empathy, or theory-of-mind capacity from observed behavior.
