# Metacognition — research grounding

> Non-clinical. This grounds the metacognition construct model. It does not profile, score, or diagnose a person.

## The model

Metacognition is monitoring and control of one's own cognition — the self-correction layer. In the ontology it reflects-on attention, memory, learning, and decision-making (it takes them as its object) and is fed by consciousness (reportable content is what it inspects). It supplies no first-order content; it watches and adjusts.

## The prediction frame

Confidence and calibration are prediction-about-prediction: a judgment of how good the system's own estimate is likely to be. Model-uncertainty work — how systems represent and report their own reliability — is the engineering parallel, and LLM-psychometrics work probes whether models hold stable, measurable self-judgments (with validity caveats). The frame keeps monitoring distinct from the thing monitored, mirroring the schema's separation of observation confidence from interpretation confidence.

## Boundary

This grounds a model of self-monitoring. It does not license rating a person's self-awareness, insight, or calibration from observed behavior.
