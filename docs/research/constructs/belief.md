# Belief — research grounding

> Non-clinical. This grounds the belief construct model. It does not profile, score, or diagnose a person.

## The model

Belief is a held model of how things are. In the ontology it is fed by memory and modulated by learning (evidence-driven update) and social-cognition (social inference shifts priors); it modulates decision-making and behavior. It is a prior that biases choice and action without supplying their content.

## The prediction frame

Belief is a prior updated by evidence — the Bayesian brain. Perception and reasoning are framed as inference: a prior meets new data, and the posterior is the revised belief. Learning modulates belief in the Bayesian-update sense, and social inference shifts the same priors. The frame stays descriptive about the mechanism and honest about its limits — real updating departs from the ideal in well-documented ways.

## Anchors

- **Bayesian approaches to brain function (active inference, free energy)** — Friston et al., review (2010). Perception and action as Bayesian inference; action selected to minimize expected free energy. Grounds the motivation/behavior/belief edges. [link](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bayesian_approaches_to_brain_function)
- **Predictive coding / the predictive brain** — Rao & Ballard; Friston; Clark, review (1999). The brain as a prediction engine minimizing prediction error across a cortical hierarchy. The cognitive-science root of the prediction frame. [link](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Predictive_coding)

## Lens alignment

Schema lenses: `bayesian-updating`, `cognitive-dissonance`, `belief-bias`, `predictive-processing`. The Bayesian-brain anchor informs `bayesian-updating`; predictive coding informs `predictive-processing`. The departure-from-ideal lenses (`cognitive-dissonance`, `belief-bias`) come from the construct module and mark where real updating diverges from the Bayesian baseline.

## Boundary

This grounds a model of how beliefs are held and revised. It does not license judging whether a person's beliefs are true, rational, or well-founded.
