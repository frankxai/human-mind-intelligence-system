# Consciousness — research grounding

> Non-clinical. This grounds the consciousness construct model. It does not profile, score, or diagnose a person.

## The model

Consciousness here means reportable (access) content — what the system can make available for report and reflection. In the ontology it is gated by attention (what reaches awareness) and it feeds metacognition (awareness is the object metacognition inspects).

## The prediction frame

This is the most contested construct, and the System stays strictly descriptive. Predictive-processing accounts treat reportable content as the substrate that downstream monitoring inspects, without resolving the hard question of phenomenal experience. The world-models survey is cited only for the access/reportability framing — a system's available latent state — not for any claim about subjective feeling. The System models access, makes no phenomenal claims, and does not adjudicate theories of consciousness.

## Anchors

- **Predictive coding / the predictive brain** — Rao & Ballard; Friston; Clark, review (1999). The brain as a prediction engine minimizing prediction error across a cortical hierarchy. The cognitive-science root of the prediction frame. [link](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Predictive_coding)
- **Understanding World or Predicting Future? A Survey of World Models** — Survey, arXiv 2411.14499 (2024). Map of the world-model landscape; situates JEPA, Cosmos, and prediction-vs-generation debates. [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.14499)

## Lens alignment

Schema lenses: `global-workspace`, `integrated-information`, `predictive-processing`. Predictive coding informs the `predictive-processing` lens; the world-models survey informs the access/reportability reading that underwrites `global-workspace` (content made globally available). `integrated-information` is named from the construct module and the System takes no position on its truth.

## Boundary

This grounds a descriptive model of reportable content. It does not license any claim about a person's inner experience, awareness level, or phenomenal state.
