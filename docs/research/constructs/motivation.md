# Motivation — research grounding

> Non-clinical. This grounds the motivation construct model. It does not profile, score, or diagnose a person.

## The model

Motivation is what gets pursued and with what force. In the ontology it is fed by emotion and identity (both modulate it) and it feeds behavior and decision-making. It is the conversion point where affect and self-model become directed effort.

## The prediction frame

Active inference selects action to minimize expected free energy rather than to chase reward directly — a prediction-first account of what gets pursued. On this view, the system acts to bring its sensory input in line with its preferred predictions, and motivation is the pull toward those preferred states. This maps the emotion→motivation and identity→motivation edges: affect and self-model both bias which predicted states count as preferred.

## Anchors

- **Bayesian approaches to brain function (active inference, free energy)** — Friston et al., review (2010). Perception and action as Bayesian inference; action selected to minimize expected free energy. Grounds the motivation/behavior/belief edges. [link](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bayesian_approaches_to_brain_function)
- **An Introduction to Predictive Processing Models of Perception and Decision-Making** — Sprevak & Smith, Topics in Cognitive Science (2024). Accessible, current synthesis of predictive processing across perception and decision-making. [link](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/tops.12704)

## Lens alignment

Schema lenses: `drive-theory`, `incentive-motivation`, `self-determination-theory`, `goal-systems`, `expectancy-value`. The active-inference anchor informs `goal-systems` (action toward preferred states) and gives a prediction-first reading of `expectancy-value`; the predictive-processing synthesis backs the same expectancy reading. The needs-based lenses (`drive-theory`, `incentive-motivation`, `self-determination-theory`) come from the construct module.

## Boundary

This grounds a model of what drives action. It does not license inferring a person's drives, values, or commitment from observed effort.
