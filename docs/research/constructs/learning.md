# Learning — research grounding

> Non-clinical. This grounds the learning construct model. It does not profile, score, or diagnose a person.

## The model

Learning is durable change from experience. In the ontology it is fed by memory and by behavior (the feedback edge — acting produces the experience to learn from), and it modulates belief and behavior in turn. It is the loop that converts repeated experience into updated dispositions.

## The prediction frame

JEPA and world foundation models learn by minimizing prediction error in latent space — the engineering of "durable change from experience." LeCun's path frames this as the core of machine intelligence; world foundation models scale it to physical environments. The behavior→learning feedback edge is exactly the loop these systems formalize: act, observe the prediction error, update. We borrow the loop's shape, not a claim that human and machine learning share mechanism.

## Anchors

- **A Path Towards Autonomous Machine Intelligence (JEPA / LV-EBM)** — Yann LeCun, OpenReview / arXiv 2306.02572 (2022). Frames machine intelligence around a world model that predicts future representations in latent space rather than generating raw output. The architectural mirror of this System's observe→predict move. [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.02572)
- **Cosmos World Foundation Model Platform for Physical AI** — NVIDIA, arXiv 2501.03575 (2025). A world foundation model: a large system trained to understand, simulate, and predict physical environments. The clearest current statement of 'model the world to predict it.' [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.03575)
- **Understanding World or Predicting Future? A Survey of World Models** — Survey, arXiv 2411.14499 (2024). Map of the world-model landscape; situates JEPA, Cosmos, and prediction-vs-generation debates. [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.14499)

## Lens alignment

Schema lenses: `forgetting-curve`, `spacing-effect`, `testing-effect`, `desirable-difficulties`, `competence-model`. These anchors inform the prediction-error framing that underwrites `desirable-difficulties` (error as the signal that drives durable change) and `competence-model`; the memory-retention lenses (`forgetting-curve`, `spacing-effect`, `testing-effect`) come from the construct module.

## Boundary

This grounds a model of how durable change accrues. It does not license rating a person's learning ability, speed, or trajectory from observed performance.
